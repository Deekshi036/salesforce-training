# CRM Basics and Salesforce Introduction

## 1. What is CRM?

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management.

CRM is a system used by companies to manage customer information, communication, sales processes, and business activities in one platform.

CRM helps companies:
- Store customer details
- Track customer interactions
- Improve customer service
- Manage sales and opportunities
- Increase business efficiency

Examples of companies using CRM:
- Colleges
- Hospitals
- Shopping companies
- Software companies

---

# 2. What is Salesforce?

Salesforce is a cloud-based CRM platform.

It helps businesses manage:
- Customers
- Sales
- Marketing
- Support services
- Business workflow

Salesforce is widely used because:
- It is easy to use
- Accessible from anywhere
- Highly customizable
- Supports automation
- Helps companies improve productivity

# 4. Salesforce Objects

Objects are database tables used to store information.

Important Salesforce objects:

## Lead
A Lead represents a person interested in a product or service.

Example:
A student interested in college admission.

## Account
An Account represents an organization or company.

Example:
ABC College

## Contact
A Contact represents a specific person connected to an account.

Example:
Student details

## Opportunity
An Opportunity represents a potential business deal or process.

Example:
Student admission process

---

# 5. Business Flow in Salesforce

Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer

## Explanation

### Lead
A person first shows interest in a service.

### Contact
After qualification, the lead becomes a contact.

### Opportunity
A business opportunity or deal is created.

### Customer
After successful completion, the person becomes a customer.
